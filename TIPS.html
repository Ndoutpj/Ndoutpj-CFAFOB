Spring Planting Guide for Vegetables

Spring
  • Prepare soil by removing weeds and adding compost
  • Check soil temperature - should be at least 50°F
  • Plant seeds according to packet instructions
  • Water gently and consistently
  • Monitor for pests and diseases

Best Time: Early spring after last frost date

Tools Needed: Shovel, Rake, Seeds, Compost

Resources: USDA Planting Calendar, Local Extension Guidelines

Natural Pest Control Methods

Summer
  • Identify specific pests affecting crops
  • Introduce beneficial insects
  • Apply neem oil spray early morning or evening
  • Use pest-repelling companion plants
  • Install physical barriers

Best Time: Early morning or evening

Tools Needed: Spray bottle, Row covers, Magnifying glass

Resources: Insect ID Chart, Pest Guide

Soil pH Testing and Amendment

Fall
  • Collect samples from various areas
  • Use test kit or lab to measure pH
  • Apply lime to raise or sulfur to lower pH
  • Recheck soil after 2–3 months

Best Time: Fall or early spring

Tools Needed: pH test kit, Lime, Sulfur, Shovel

Resources: Soil Lab Directory, Crop pH Chart

Water Conservation Techniques

Summer
  • Install drip irrigation systems
  • Apply mulch to retain soil moisture
  • Water deeply but less frequently
  • Use rain gauges and barrels

Best Time: Morning to reduce evaporation

Tools Needed: Drip irrigation kit, Rain barrel, Mulch

Resources: Water-Wise Guide, Irrigation Manual

Companion Planting Strategies

Spring
  • Research compatible plant combinations
  • Plant nitrogen-fixing legumes
  • Use aromatic herbs to repel pests
  • Rotate companion plant locations

Best Time: Regular planting season

Tools Needed: Garden planner, Seeds, Measuring tape

Resources: Companion Planting Chart

Composting for Healthy Soil

Fall
  • Set up compost bin in shaded area
  • Layer green and brown materials
  • Maintain moisture level
  • Turn compost every 2–3 weeks

Best Time: Year-round; fall best for browns

Tools Needed: Compost bin, Pitchfork, Thermometer

Resources: Compost Basics, Troubleshooting Guide

Winter Crop Protection

Winter
  • Monitor weather forecasts
  • Cover sensitive plants
  • Use water-filled containers for heat
  • Apply thick mulch

Best Time: Before frost and throughout winter

Tools Needed: Row covers, Blankets, Mulch

Resources: Frost Protection Methods

Seed Starting Indoors

Winter
  • Calculate planting dates
  • Use sterile seed mix
  • Provide consistent light
  • Harden off seedlings

Best Time: 6–8 weeks before last frost

Tools Needed: Seed trays, Grow lights, Heat mat

Resources: Seed Starting Calendar
